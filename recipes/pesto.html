Spaghetti Cacio e Pepe!

Pesto is one of the most popular pasta toppings in Italy. It originated in Liguria, specifically in Genoa.(

Shis flavorful sauce is made of basil, extra-virgin olive oil, parmesan cheese, pecorino cheese, pine nuts, garlic and salt, traditionally ground in a ceramic or marble mortar.

The most authentic way to make pesto is with a mortar and pestle, although a blender or food processor is often used to speed things up.

Ingredients:

  • 240g of Spaghetti or Spaghettoni
  • 120g of Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 150g of Grana Padano cheese
  • 20g of Olive oil
  • 5g of Corn starch
  • 20g cracked Black pepper
  • 50ml of water
    •

    How to do it

    1. First fill a pot with water and a tablespoon of salt and put it into a boil and let the spaghetti cook for about 10 to 12 minutes in the boiling water, or as your spaghetti package says so
    2. Mix the corn starch with the 50ml of water and put it the pan on low heat and let it reduce until you got a gel like consistency
    3. Witch the help of a food processor, mix the corn starch gel with both cheeses, olive oil and the cracked black pepper until you got a cream cheese like consistency
    4. When the spaghetti is about to get cooked, put your the mixture of the previous step on a pan with a little of pasta water on low heat and mix it with the spaghetti
